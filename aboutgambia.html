Good to Know

Smallest Country in Africa

Less than 30 miles wide. The Gambia follows The River Gambia, bordered on either side of Senegal. But surprisingly populated with approx 2.28m people.

Home to Nine Tribes

The oldest and most popular is the Mandinkas, along with Fula and Wolof. All living together peacefully and each preserving their own language, music and traditions.

Bird Lover Paradise

If you're an avid Twitcher we strongly recommend bringing your camera. With 540 species of birds covering forests & banks. Up River, you'll find hippos and several species of Monkeys.

50 mile Coastline

Considering its size, The Gambia boasts an impressive stretch of golden sands. With year-round sunshine and just a 6 hr flight from Europe, it makes for the perfect beach holiday.

UNESCO Heritage Site

In 2003, Kunta Kinte Island became a UNESCO site. The best-seller 'Roots' highlights this historical location. Visited by travelling up River, where you will also find an educational museum about the transatlantic slave trade.

How to Get There

By Air

Travelling to The Gambia takes only a short time, considering the tropical climates. Major airlines that fly weekly: Brussels Airlnes (Brussels to Banjul), Vueling (Barcelona to Banjul), Gambia Experience (London to Banjul - via Senegal), AirMaroc (Morocco to Banjul). Banjul International Airport is located in Old Yundum, just 30 minutes drive to Senegambia and 40 minutes to Fajara.

By Road

With Senegalese borders all around The Gambia, travelling by road is a popular choice. The journey can take up to 8 hours, going through several suburban towns and villages. For a shorter trip, it's recommended taking the ferry from Barra to Banjul. The longer route is to use the Trans-Gambia Highway which leads you north-south of the country. On to Senegal.
