Practical Information

Money

USD, GBP, EUR are all accepted. If you want to use the local curreny (GMD) for ease, you will want to bring your own currency to convert when you arrive. There are many Western Unions available to do this. Alternatively you can use your card (visa/debit) at any of the ATMs.

Language

Wolof, Mandinka and Jolla are all spoken widely in the Gambia, as many locals descend from either three of these tribes. However due to Gambia being an ex-English colony, many Gambians are well versed in English. So you won't have a problem conversing, although we do recommend learning some of the basic tongue. Not to mention it will be very weel appreciated

Seasons

Most travellers visit between November-April, a.k.a 'winter season'. However, weather in Gambia is enjoyable throughout the year. Only in August and September can it be humid, but if you like tropical storms and beautiful green surroundings, then it's perfect.

Driving

The Gambia is currently undergoing major infrastructure changes and with only one main highway from Banjul, traffic can be somewhat overwhelming. We recommend if hiring a car, to make sure you avoid main roads at peak times make sure to give yourself plenty of time when getting from a to b.
